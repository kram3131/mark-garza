THE SHARED STYLE BLOCK
======================
Prepend this to EVERY image prompt, and attach the mascot (assets/fox.png)
as the style reference on every gen after the mascot itself. This one
paragraph is what makes all 17 plates read as a single hand.

---
Storybook cartographer's field-journal illustration, watercolor and fine
brown ink linework on white paper, warm sepia base, soft irregular
watercolor edges fading to plain white paper, delicate cross-hatching,
matte flat scan look. No text, no letters, no people, no animals, no
military insignia, no crests, no logos, no flags.
---

Two notes on that block:
- "No animals" applies to the terrain and the props only. The mascot (the
  fox) is the ONE animal — it's generated first, on its own, as the style
  anchor, then attached as the reference to everything else.
- The white background is deliberate. Every plate is generated on plain
  white, then run through bake_alpha.py (color-to-alpha) so the paint sits
  inked onto the map and the survey grid + route road still breathe through.
